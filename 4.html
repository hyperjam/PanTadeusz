If you these with amazing great bravery subdue Then even more dire perils are waiting for you: There lie in wait, like wolf-pits, that dare you to pass, Little pools half grown over with mat of rank grass So very deep, that no man can fathom such pit, (And it is very likely, therein devils sit). Water in these shafts glistens, with bloody rust spotted, And steam from the depths rises, with smell of things rotted, Which makes the trees that grow there lose all leaf and bark; Bald and dwarfish, worm-riddled, unhealthy and stark, With mossy tangled elf-locks from crooked limbs hanging, And stumps hump-backed, and bristling with unlovely fungi, Squat like a witches' coven round a cauldron wheezing, Their hands warming, while in it a fresh corpse is seething.
